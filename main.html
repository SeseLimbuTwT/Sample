POTATOES

Potatoes are one of the world’s most beloved and versatile vegetables. Originally cultivated in the Andes mountains of South America, they are now a staple food across the globe. Packed with nutrients, potatoes are an excellent source of vitamin C, potassium, fiber, and complex carbohydrates that provide long-lasting energy.

From crispy golden fries and creamy mashed potatoes to hearty soups and baked dishes, their mild flavor and fluffy or waxy textures make them perfect for countless recipes. Popular varieties include Russet, Yukon Gold, red potatoes, and purple potatoes — each offering unique taste and texture profiles suited for different cooking methods.

Beyond their delicious taste, potatoes are naturally gluten-free, fat-free, and budget-friendly, making them a nutritious choice for families and chefs alike. Whether roasted, boiled, baked, or fried, potatoes continue to be a comforting and essential ingredient in cuisines around the world.

Types of Potatoes

  • Russet

    • Russet potatoes are large, oblong potatoes with rough brown skin and white flesh, known for their high starch content and low moisture. This makes them light and fluffy on the inside when cooked, with a crispy exterior when baked or fried. They are commonly used for baked potatoes, French fries, mashed potatoes, and roasting because they absorb flavors well and develop a soft texture. Nutritionally, russet potatoes provide carbohydrates, fiber (especially in the skin), vitamin C, vitamin B6, and potassium, and they are naturally fat-free and cholesterol-free.
